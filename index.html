Expert Techniques

Word Game Strategies That Win

Beyond brute-force solving, our tool teaches you the mental frameworks that top word-game players use every day.

In Scrabble and similar games, certain letter pairings unlock long words. Combinations like QU, TH, SH, CH, PH, WR and suffix clusters like -TION, -ING, -ED, -ER dramatically expand what you can form from a given rack.
When you know some positions in a crossword answer, use our pattern field: enter known letters in their positions and ? for blanks. A pattern like ?E?T will instantly return all four-letter words matching that structure.
After each Wordle guess, use the "Exclude Letters" filter to remove grey letters. Combine with "Must Include" for yellow letters and the pattern for green letters. This narrows 12,000+ five-letter words down to a handful in two or three iterations.
Our results are sorted longest-first because longer words use more letters and score more points. Scan from the top: if a 7-letter word fits, play it. This is especially powerful in Scrabble where seven-letter words earn a 50-point bonus.
Two-letter words like QI, ZA, AX, JO, XI, AA are legal in most word games and are invaluable for parallel plays. Filter by "Max Length: 2" and memorise the list — it's one of the highest-return investments a word gamer can make.

Word Puzzle Glossary

Anagram A word formed by rearranging all the letters of another word. "LISTEN" → "SILENT".
Unscramble Finding valid words from a set of mixed-up letters, not necessarily using all of them.
Rack The set of tiles held by a player in Scrabble or similar games (usually 7 tiles).
Pattern A word template with known letters and placeholders (?) for unknown positions.
Blank Tile A wildcard tile (represented as ?) that can substitute for any letter in the alphabet.
Bingo Using all 7 tiles in a single Scrabble turn, earning a 50-point bonus.
Hook Adding a letter to an existing word on the Scrabble board to form a new word.
